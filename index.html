PROB 01 · Lec 5 — Freezing Point Depression
المسألة
What is the freezing point of a 0.05 molal solution of a non-electrolyte in water?
(Kf = 1.86 °C/m)
ΔTf = Kf × m
Given: m = 0.05 mol/kg, Kf = 1.86 °C/m

ΔTf = Kf × m
ΔTf = 1.86 × 0.05
ΔTf = 0.093 °C

Tf(solution) = Tf(solvent) − ΔTf
Tf = 0 − 0.093
✓ Tf = −0.093 °C
PROB 02 · Lec 5 — Osmotic Pressure
المسألة
Calculate the osmotic pressure of a 0.300 M solution of glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) in water at 37°C.
(R = 0.082 atm·L·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹)
π = MRT
Given: M = 0.300 mol/L
R = 0.082 atm·L·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹
T = 37 + 273 = 310 K

π = M × R × T
π = 0.300 × 0.082 × 310
π = 0.300 × 25.42
✓ π = 7.626 atm
PROB 03 · Lec 5 — Boiling Point Elevation
المسألة
A solution contains 5.0 g of glucose (MW = 180 g/mol) dissolved in 100 g of water.
Calculate the boiling point of the solution. (Kb = 0.512 °C/m, normal bp of water = 100.0 °C)
m = moles solute / kg solvent  |  ΔTb = Kb × m
moles of glucose = 5.0 / 180 = 0.02778 mol
kg of water = 100 / 1000 = 0.1 kg

m = 0.02778 / 0.1 = 0.2778 mol/kg

ΔTb = Kb × m
ΔTb = 0.512 × 0.2778
ΔTb = 0.1422 °C

Tb(solution) = 100.0 + 0.1422
✓ Tb = 100.14 °C
PROB 04 · Lec 5 — Electrolyte Effect
المسألة
Calculate the freezing point depression of a 0.1 molal BaCl₂ solution in water.
Assume complete ionization. (Kf = 1.86 °C/m)
BaCl₂ → Ba²⁺ + 2Cl⁻ (3 ions)  |  ΔTf = i × Kf × m
BaCl₂ → Ba²⁺ + 2Cl⁻ = 3 ions total (i = 3)

ΔTf = i × Kf × m
ΔTf = 3 × 1.86 × 0.1
ΔTf = 0.558 °C

Tf = 0 − 0.558
✓ Tf = −0.558 °C
PROB 05 · Lec 6&7 — Isotopes & Configuration
المسألة
Carbon-14 (¹⁴₆C):
a) How many protons, neutrons, and electrons does it have?
b) Write its electronic configuration.
c) Is it an isotope of ¹²₆C? Why?
a) Atomic number Z = 6 → Protons = 6
Mass number A = 14 → Neutrons = 14 − 6 = 8
Neutral atom → Electrons = 6

b) Electronic configuration of ⁶C:
1s² 2s² 2p²

c) Yes — ¹²C and ¹⁴C both have Z=6 (same element)
but different neutron numbers (6 vs 8) → Isotopes ✓
✓ Protons=6, Neutrons=8, e⁻=6 | Config: 1s² 2s² 2p²
